The desert at night is a completely different world. By day, Saguaro National Park is defined by heat, harsh light, and the relentless survival of its flora. But once the sun drops below the horizon, the desert breathes a sigh of relief, and a profound silence settles over the landscape.

I set up my tripod in the darkness, guided only by the soft glow of my red headlamp to preserve my night vision.

## Echoes of the cosmos

Standing next to a thirty-foot-tall Saguaro that has lived for over a century makes you feel incredibly small. Looking up at the band of the Milky Way arching directly over its arms makes you feel even smaller. 

The air in the Sonoran Desert is dry and remarkably clear. As my eyes adjusted, the sheer volume of stars became overwhelming. I set my camera to a 20-second exposure, opened the lens wide, and waited.

Each exposure revealed details the human eye can only hint at—the subtle purples and dust clouds of the galactic center, framed perfectly by the ancient, reaching arms of the desert giants.
