There is a specific stretch of Highway 163 that everyone recognizes, even if they've never been to Arizona or Utah. It’s the point where the road stretches out straight as an arrow, dipping down before rising directly into the towering, majestic monoliths of Monument Valley.

When you drive over that crest, the scale of the landscape hits you all at once.

## Monuments of the past

These massive structures—the Mittens, Merrick Butte, Sentinel Mesa—rise up to 1,000 feet above the valley floor. They are remnants of a vast plateau that has slowly eroded over eons, leaving behind these striking monuments of sandstone and shale.

I parked the car on the shoulder, stepped out into the warm wind, and just looked. 

There is an incredible sense of timelessness here. The road is a modern addition, but the valley itself feels completely untouched by the passage of years. It’s a landscape that demands you slow down, roll down the windows, and let the dust of the road become a part of the journey.
