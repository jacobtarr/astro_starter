The North Rim of the Grand Canyon feels like a completely different park. Located a thousand feet higher than its bustling southern counterpart, it is blanketed in dense forests of aspen and ponderosa pine.

I arrived at Bright Angel Point just as the first grey light of dawn was beginning to separate the sky from the canyon.

## Chasing the shadows

At this altitude, the air is thin and crisp. As the sun rises, the light doesn't hit the canyon floor immediately. Instead, it skims across the top of the plateau, igniting the highest ridges while leaving the deep chasms in a state of ink-blue mystery.

The sheer scale of the canyon is hard to compress into a viewfinder. I focused my lens on the layers—how the sandstone shelves stack like cards, disappearing into the morning haze.

By the time the sun fully cleared the horizon, the deep reds and purples of the limestone began to show. It is a slow, quiet reveal that rewards those who wake up early enough to witness it.
