There is a stretch of dirt road just past Tortilla Flat that makes you feel like you've slipped back in time. The Apache Trail is narrow, winding, and completely unforgiving, hugging the sides of sheer canyon walls.

I pulled over at a small turnout overlooking Canyon Lake, watching the shadows creep over the water.

## The glow of the Superstitions

The Superstition Mountains have a reputation for being mysterious, and it’s easy to see why. As the sun drops, the volcanic rock faces seem to absorb the warm light, turning a deep, bruised purple before dissolving into the night.

The key to photographing this landscape is patience. The sky changes rapidly from yellow to a deep, burning magenta.

I set up my camera to capture a lone saguaro standing on the edge of the ridge, silhouetted against the vibrant gradient of the sky. In the desert, sometimes the simplest compositions are the ones that tell the most powerful story.
