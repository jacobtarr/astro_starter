Nothing quite prepares you for the sheer scale of Horseshoe Bend. You walk up a sandy trail in Page, Arizona, feeling the dry heat radiate from the ground, and suddenly the earth simply opens up in front of you.

There is no guardrail here in the spots where the views are best. Just a vertical drop of a thousand feet to the river below.

## A ribbon of green

From the rim, the Colorado River looks like a calm, emerald ribbon wrapping itself around a massive sandstone tower. The water has carved this dramatic 270-degree bend over millions of years, cutting deep into the Navajo sandstone layers.

I sat near the edge, letting my feet dangle over the void—a little terrifying, but entirely mesmerizing. 

As the sun began to dip, the canyon walls turned deep shades of ochre and rust. The shadows climbed up from the riverbed, slowly swallowing the green water until only the very top of the central rock formation remained illuminated, a golden crown in a sea of shadow.
